Son of the Soil

"I did not read about rural hardship. I lived it."

Born into a small farmer family and raised by hard-working, labour-based parents, I grew up with the everyday realities of rural Telangana — limited opportunity, economic hardship and unequal access.

My education came through government schools and social-welfare hostels. That upbringing is not a line on a profile — it is the lens through which I understand the people of this constituency, because I am one of them.

Movement Identity

"The movement taught me what equal dignity is worth."

The Telangana movement was a defining chapter in my public life. It deepened my understanding of regional inequality, educational and healthcare gaps, unemployment, and the long struggle for identity, self-respect and equitable development.

In 2011 I helped build a movement-oriented social platform — turning conviction into organised, grassroots engagement years before it became a formal organisation.

Where I Choose to Serve

"This is the soil that raised me — and the people I intend to serve."

Sirpur–Kaghaznagar is not a constituency I chose. It is the place that made me — its villages, its farmers, its tribal hamlets, its working families and its young people looking for a future.

I know its struggles because they were mine: schools that needed more, healthcare that was too far, and opportunity that too often passed our youth by. I return not as a stranger, but as a son ready to give back.
