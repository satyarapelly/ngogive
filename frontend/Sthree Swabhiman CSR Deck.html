Sthree Swabhiman 02 · Executive Summary
Executive Summary

A government-partnered programme to end period absenteeism, shame, and unsafe disposal across 275 campuses.

Sthree Swabhiman is a structured Menstrual Hygiene Management initiative for adolescent girls in MJPTBCWREIS residential schools and junior colleges across Telangana — combining annual awareness, sanitary napkin starter kits, permanent incinerator infrastructure, and Training of Trainers.

The programme is endorsed by a Memorandum of Collaboration with the Secretary, MJPTBCWREIS, and a pilot launched in November 2025 in Warangal reached 1,200+ students.

03 · The Problem
The Problem

For a girl in a rural residential school, her period costs her five school days every month.

Across MJPTBCWREIS campuses, menstruation collides with three failures at once: no awareness, no reliable products, and nowhere safe to dispose of waste. The result is silence, shame, and absence.

Sthree Swabhiman 05 · Our Response
Our Response

A four-part programme, delivered annually, designed to graduate to institutional self-reliance by Year 3.

Pillar 01 · Awareness

Structured MHM sessions in every institution, every year.

One-day workshops on menstrual cycle, hygiene, myth-busting, and emotional well-being — delivered in Telugu.

275 sessions / yr
Pillar 02 · Access

Starter kits placed in the hands of every newly-enrolled girl.

12 sanitary napkin packs per girl, with a user guide and demonstration on safe disposal practice.

88,000 kits / yr
Pillar 03 · Infrastructure

Permanent eco-friendly incinerators on every campus.

Two units per institution — a one-time capital investment with a 10+ year service life and on-site training.

550 units installed
Pillar 04 · Capacity

Training of Trainers, so the programme outlives our presence.

Lady wardens, science teachers, and peer-mentor students are certified to deliver content independently.

825+ trained

Sthree Swabhiman 09 · Sustainability Model
Sustainability Model

By Year 3, every campus runs the programme on its own — without NGO dependency.

Year Give For Society Institution
1 Full implementation. Sessions, kits, incinerators, ToT delivered end-to-end. Observe, nominate focal persons, host facilitation.
2 Partial support. Joint sessions co-delivered with institution staff. Lead delivery with NGO guidance; manage incinerators independently.
3 Remote only. Refresher content and technical helpline. Run programme fully independently; maintain infrastructure.

The investment lasts beyond the funding year. Incinerators have a 10+ year service life. IEC materials are reusable. Trained staff stay on campus. Peer mentors graduate into the next cohort.

→No recurring product procurement after Year 1 starter kit.
→Self-financing pipeline routed through MJPTBCWREIS allocations.
→Programme survives staff transfers via documented curriculum.
